# Safety & Compliance

## Content Policy Per Platform

### Instagram/Meta
- No nudity, sexual content, or sexually suggestive poses
- No violence, gore, or graphic injury content
- No hate speech, discrimination, harassment, or bullying
- No misinformation about health, elections, vaccines, or public safety
- AI-generated content must be labeled (Meta's AI disclosure requirement effective 2024)
- No impersonation of real people without verifiable consent
- No promotion of regulated goods (firearms, drugs, tobacco) without restrictions
- Copyright: no reposting without credit/permission; Content Rights Manager enforces
- Branded content: must use "Paid Partnership" tag for sponsored posts
- Engagement bait (like/comment farming) is penalized algorithmically

### TikTok
- Community guidelines aligned with Meta but stricter on:
  - Dangerous activities, stunts, or challenges that could cause harm
  - Minor safety (no content featuring minors in unsafe situations)
  - Synthetic media — deepfakes and AI-generated faces must be disclosed
  - Misinformation — fact-checking partnerships actively review flagged content
- AI content disclosure required since 2024 (toggle in upload settings)
- Music: only use licensed sounds from the TikTok Commercial Music Library for business accounts
- No unauthorized use of copyrighted audio on business/ad content
- Restricted categories: gambling, alcohol, financial services (require pre-approval)
- Account integrity: no artificial engagement (bots, follow-for-follow schemes)

### YouTube
- Advertiser-friendly content guidelines (critical for monetization):
  - No profanity in the first 7 seconds of a video
  - No excessive profanity throughout (limited monetization)
  - No controversial or sensitive topics without educational context
  - No violence without appropriate context and framing
  - No drug-related content (even discussion) without educational framing
- AI-generated content: must disclose in video description and settings panel
- Realistic AI content depicting real people: requires disclosure or removal
- COPPA compliance: content directed at children has restricted data collection and no targeted ads
- Copyright: Content ID system automatically scans and flags matching content
- Fair use: transformative, commentary, criticism, education — but YouTube errs on the side of claimants
- Repeat strikes: 3 copyright strikes = channel termination
- Community Guidelines strikes: 3 in 90 days = channel termination

### X/Twitter
- Synthetic media policy: must label AI-generated content, especially realistic depictions of real people
- No manipulated media intended to deceive or cause harm
- Hateful conduct policy: no attacks based on protected categories
- Sensitive media: can post with content warning, not in profile/header images
- Less restrictive than Meta/TikTok overall on content types
- No spam, platform manipulation, or coordinated inauthentic behavior
- Paid promotion: must disclose with appropriate labeling

## AI-Generated Content Disclosure

### Platform-Specific Requirements

**Meta/Instagram:**
- Auto-labeling system detects AI-generated content using C2PA metadata and invisible watermarks
- Manual disclosure: add "Made with AI" label when uploading
- Failure to disclose may result in Meta adding the label automatically
- Penalties for deliberate concealment of AI origin on sensitive topics

**TikTok:**
- Toggle "AI-generated content" in upload settings (mandatory)
- AIGC label appears on the video for viewers
- Content featuring realistic AI-generated people requires label even if satirical
- Enforcement: content removal and account warnings for non-disclosure

**YouTube:**
- Check "Altered or synthetic content" in Content > Advanced Settings
- Required for: realistic depictions of real people, realistic events, realistic settings
- Not required for: clearly fantastical/animated content, color correction, beauty filters
- Penalty for non-disclosure: content removal, strike, monetization suspension

**X/Twitter:**
- Add context in post text or description
- No enforced toggle yet, but policy requires disclosure
- Realistic synthetic media of real people without label may be removed

### Best Practice
Always disclose AI-generated content regardless of whether the platform technically requires it. This builds audience trust, protects against future policy changes, and demonstrates ethical content creation practices.

### Disclosure Language Templates
- "Visuals created with AI" (minimal)
- "This video was produced using AI-generated imagery and voice synthesis" (detailed)
- "AI-assisted content — images generated by [model name]" (transparent)

## Copyright & Fair Use

### AI-Generated Image Copyright
- **United States**: USPTO and Copyright Office position — purely AI-generated images (no human authorship) are not copyrightable. Human-directed AI art with sufficient creative control may qualify.
- **European Union**: varies by member state; generally requires human creative contribution for copyright
- **Practical guidance**: treat AI-generated content as if it has limited copyright protection; add human creative elements (editing, composition, overlay) to strengthen claims

### Stock Media Licensing
- Verify license type before commercial use:
  - **Royalty-free**: pay once, use multiple times, some restrictions
  - **Rights-managed**: licensed per specific use case, more restrictions
  - **Creative Commons**: various levels (CC0 = public domain, CC-BY = attribution required, CC-BY-NC = non-commercial only)
  - **Editorial only**: cannot be used in commercial/advertising contexts
- Always check: commercial use allowed, modification allowed, attribution required

### Music Licensing
- Only use royalty-free or properly licensed tracks
- Platform-specific music libraries:
  - TikTok Commercial Music Library (business accounts)
  - YouTube Audio Library (free for YouTube use)
  - Meta Sound Collection (free for Meta platforms)
- Third-party royalty-free sources: Epidemic Sound, Artlist, Uppbeat
- Never use copyrighted music without a sync license
- AI-generated music: copyright status unclear — use with caution for commercial content

### User-Generated Content
- Get written permission (DM confirmation minimum, formal release preferred) before repurposing
- Credit the original creator always
- Respect "do not repost" requests immediately
- UGC in ads requires explicit written consent and potentially compensation

### Fair Use Factors (US)
1. **Purpose**: transformative, commentary, criticism, education, parody (favors fair use)
2. **Nature**: factual works more likely fair use than creative/fictional
3. **Amount**: using small portions favors fair use; using the "heart" of the work does not
4. **Market effect**: if your use substitutes for purchasing the original, not fair use

## Brand Safety Guardrails

### Pre-Publish Checklist
```
[ ] 1. No offensive, controversial, or potentially harmful imagery
[ ] 2. Brand colors and fonts correctly applied per guidelines
[ ] 3. No competitor logos, trademarks, or products visible
[ ] 4. Tone and voice match brand guidelines (casual/professional/playful)
[ ] 5. All claims and statistics are verifiable with sources
[ ] 6. Contact info, links, and CTAs are correct and functional
[ ] 7. No personal data exposed (faces, addresses, phone numbers, emails)
[ ] 8. AI-generated content properly disclosed per platform requirements
[ ] 9. Captions/subtitles present and accurate
[ ] 10. Alt text written for accessibility
[ ] 11. Correct aspect ratio and resolution for target platform
[ ] 12. Hashtags reviewed (no hijacked or inappropriate tags)
[ ] 13. Posting time optimized for audience activity
[ ] 14. Approved by designated reviewer (if approval workflow active)
```

### Automated Pre-Publish Checks
- Run content through moderation API before scheduling
- Automated spell/grammar check on all text content
- Image quality validation (resolution, aspect ratio, file size)
- Link validation (no broken URLs)
- Brand color compliance check (dominant colors match palette)
- Profanity/slur detection in captions and text overlays

### Brand Crisis Protocol
1. Immediately pause all scheduled content
2. Assess the situation and gather facts
3. Remove problematic content if applicable
4. Draft response aligned with brand values
5. Communicate transparently with audience
6. Document incident and update guardrails to prevent recurrence

## NSFW Detection

### OpenAI Moderation Endpoint
```
POST https://api.openai.com/v1/moderations
Body: {
  "input": "text to check"
}
Response categories: sexual, hate, harassment, self-harm, violence,
                     sexual/minors, hate/threatening, violence/graphic
Each category returns: flagged (bool), score (0.0-1.0)
```

### Google Cloud Vision SafeSearch
```
POST https://vision.googleapis.com/v1/images:annotate
Body: {
  "requests": [{
    "image": {"source": {"imageUri": "https://..."}},
    "features": [{"type": "SAFE_SEARCH_DETECTION"}]
  }]
}
Response: adult, spoof, medical, violence, racy — each rated:
  VERY_UNLIKELY, UNLIKELY, POSSIBLE, LIKELY, VERY_LIKELY
```

### Custom Keyword Blocklists
- Maintain platform-specific blocklists for captions, hashtags, and text overlays
- Categories: profanity, slurs, controversial topics, competitor names, regulated terms
- Auto-flag content containing blocklisted terms for human review
- Update blocklists monthly based on emerging terms and cultural shifts

### Automated Flagging Workflow
```
Content Created → Moderation API Check →
  IF flagged → Queue for Human Review → Approve / Reject / Edit
  IF clean → Proceed to Pre-Publish Checklist → Schedule
```

## Accessibility Requirements

### Captions and Subtitles
- **Required** for all video content across all platforms
- Auto-generate with OpenAI Whisper API or platform auto-captions
- **Always review** for accuracy, especially: proper nouns, brand names, technical terms, numbers
- Burn-in (hardcode) captions for platforms without native caption support (Instagram Reels, TikTok)
- Upload as separate SRT/VTT track when platform supports it (YouTube)
- Caption timing: sync within 0.5 seconds of spoken audio

### Caption Styling
- Font: clean sans-serif (Arial, Helvetica, or brand font if readable)
- Size: minimum 32px at 1080p resolution
- Color: white text with black outline (2-3px) or semi-transparent black background box
- Position: lower third of frame, avoid covering faces or important visuals
- Max 2 lines on screen at once
- Reading speed: maximum 200 words per minute

### Alt Text for Images
- Write for all images posted to any platform
- Describe the content and function, not just "image of product"
- Good: "A person holding the BrandX water bottle while hiking on a mountain trail at sunset"
- Bad: "Product image" or "Photo"
- Include relevant details: actions, emotions, setting, text visible in image
- Length: 125 characters max for Twitter, 1000 characters for Instagram

### Audio Descriptions
- For visual-only content (demonstrations, tutorials, product reveals)
- Describe key visual information not conveyed through existing audio
- Can be added as a secondary audio track or narration

### Color Contrast
- WCAG AA minimum: 4.5:1 contrast ratio for normal text, 3:1 for large text
- Test all text overlays against their backgrounds
- Do not rely on color alone to convey information
- Tools: WebAIM Contrast Checker, Colour Contrast Analyser

## GDPR/Privacy

### Personal Data in Content
- No personal data (names, faces, contact info) without explicit consent
- Consent must be: freely given, specific, informed, unambiguous
- Document consent with signed model releases or written agreements
- Minors: require parental/guardian consent, stricter protections

### Face and Identity Protection
- Blur faces of non-consenting individuals in photos and videos
- Get signed model releases for any recognizable person in commercial content
- AI-generated faces: disclose as synthetic (do not present as real individuals)
- Consider cultural sensitivity around photographing people in different regions

### Metadata and Location Data
- Strip EXIF metadata (GPS coordinates, device info, timestamps) before publishing
- Tools: ExifTool, ImageMagick, or platform-specific stripping
- Do not geolocate content at private addresses
- Be cautious with location tags that reveal sensitive areas

### User Comments and DMs
- Never share user comments or DM conversations publicly without anonymization
- Screenshots of conversations: remove names, profile pictures, and identifying details
- UGC compilations: get explicit permission from each contributor

### Data Retention
- Define how long user-submitted content (UGC, contest entries) is stored
- Set automated deletion schedules for expired campaigns
- Document retention policies in privacy policy
- Regularly audit stored data against retention schedule

### Right to Erasure
- Process for handling content removal requests:
  1. Acknowledge request within 24 hours
  2. Verify identity of requester
  3. Remove content from all platforms within 30 days (GDPR requirement)
  4. Remove from internal storage and backups
  5. Confirm removal to requester
- Maintain a log of all erasure requests and actions taken

## Rate Limits & Fair Usage

### API Rate Limit Compliance
- Never circumvent or bypass API rate limits
- Implement exponential backoff with jitter for retries:
  ```
  wait_time = min(base_delay * 2^attempt + random_jitter, max_delay)
  ```
- Monitor rate limit headers in API responses (`X-RateLimit-Remaining`, `Retry-After`)
- Queue requests and process within limits rather than bursting

### Platform Posting Frequency
- **Instagram**: 1-2 feed posts/day, 3-5 stories/day, 4-7 reels/week
- **TikTok**: 1-3 posts/day (more frequent posting is rewarded)
- **YouTube**: 1-2 videos/week (consistency matters more than frequency)
- **YouTube Shorts**: 1-2/day
- **X/Twitter**: 3-5 tweets/day (including replies and retweets)
- **LinkedIn**: 1 post/day, 3-5/week optimal

### Anti-Spam Guidelines
- Do not post identical content across multiple accounts simultaneously
- Space out cross-platform posts by at least 1-2 hours
- Vary captions and hashtags even for cross-posted content
- Do not use automation to mass-follow, mass-like, or mass-comment
- Respect platform-specific automation policies (Instagram is strictest)

### Fair Usage Principles
- Use APIs for their intended purpose
- Do not scrape platforms outside of official API access
- Respect robots.txt and terms of service
- Report bugs and vulnerabilities responsibly
- Contribute to platform ecosystem health (quality content, genuine engagement)
